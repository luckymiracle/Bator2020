Welcome to Ria's Incubator

Active Pasts Lucky Miracle

My dad and I made an incubator to hatch chicken eggs. Hatching chicken eggs requires the right temperature and humidity. The eggs also need to be rotated. The temperature should remain between 99F-103F. To keep the temperature this high, the incubator must be well insulated. We used thermal insulation and plexi glass. If it goes higher or lower for an hour it might decrease hatching rate. If it stays a few degrees higher for hours then it will probably decrease the hatching rate and might make the ones who hatch physically deformed (missing a toe or more than four toes).

Our incubator is programmed to switch a light bulb on and off to keep the temperature between 99.5F and 100.5F. Most people say the humidity should be 45% to 55% for the first 18 days and 65% for the last 3 days. But a mother hen keeps the humidity at 75% to 80%. Our incubator is programmed to keep the humidity around 75% by using a vent controlled by a servo and a humidifier we made.
